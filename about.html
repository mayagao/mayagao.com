I was born in a small beach town in East China. I went to college for western philosophy, before coming to the US in 2012 for a master’s program in multimedia journalism.


At grad school, I started a few startup projects with my friends, including a food delivery website, an iPad tooth brushing game for kids, and a web application that can analyze people’s interactions with friends on Facebook. The need for endless iterations and constant launches allows me to learn a lot of things, from user research, visual design to prototyping, within a short period of time. I never received any systematic design education and I started learning design by just doing it.


I've always been intrigued by the challenge of finding innovative ways to present large amounts of information and data to the public. And I was lucky enough to start my career at a design agency, Upstatement, that’s exactly about tackling that challenge: they empower journalism and storytellers. I contributed to the QA and debugging process for several clients including Nieman Journalism Foundation and Harvard Law Review. I also designed content management tools to simplify the publishing and editing process for news organizations. I created the concept, designed the interface, and also wrote part of the front-end code. It's the first time I was designing systems that can solve problems for real people, not just web pages that’s constrained inside a browser window, and I absolutely loved it. The most important lesson I learned while working there is to think small: instead of trying to find the perfect strategy, start from solving small, urgent problems for the users.


Right now I am a user interface designer at Lonely Planet, a company that makes a variety of print and digital products for travellers. Here, I work with external clients like Delta, Budget, and JEEP to create partnership campaigns or other digital products using LP content. I am involved in all stages of the product process—from concept (Like creating campaign positioning and messaging) to launch—in collaboration with a global team of content producers, product managers, and engineers. In addition, I also contributed to improving the user experience of the LP website, by making small adjustments to a page or a user flow with A/B testing, or overhauling a whole system. Here at LP I am surrounded by passionate people from all around the world with various culture backgrounds. I learned from them, not just design techniques, but also how to respect, listen, show empathy, and stay unique.


That’s all about me, and it has been an amazing journey so far. I think the biggest pleasure of being a designer is creating tools that can make an impact on people’s daily life. The challenging process of finding the simplest solution to problems within constraints always excites me.
